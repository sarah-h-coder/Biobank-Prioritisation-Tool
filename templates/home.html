Biobank Prioritisation Tool

Prioritising species for samples collection and storage in biobanks

Background on the project

Biobanks have been established to safeguard existing genetic diversity. They freeze biological animal material that can later be defrosted and used to reintroduce genetic diversity into ex-situ and in-situ populations. For example, artificial insemination of cryopreserved spermatozoa in the endangered black-footed ferret (Mustela nigripes) has recovered their population in-situ. Unfortunately, biobanks face challenges such as high operational costs and limited funding, and must prioritise their efforts.

The tool

This tool has been develop to help biobanks prioritise species to collect samples from. It takes into account the species conservation value (IUCN Category, CITES Appendix, ED Score), the demand (requests) for samples from that species, and the number of samples already present within the biobank. The tool uses the MAPISCo methodology (Defra, UK) which involves converting string data to numeric data, taking the mean of invdividual scores within a category, calculating the categories z-score, applying a weight to this score, and then taking the mean of the weighted categories to produce a priority score that ranks species.

Limitations

The tool is problematic with handling missing data, it assigns values of zero to missing data which artificially reduced the overall priority scores, therefore penalising species missing data.
